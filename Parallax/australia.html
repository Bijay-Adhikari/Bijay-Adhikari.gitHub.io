Why study in Australia?

Australia is a popular destination for international students because of its high-quality education system, multicultural society, and excellent standard of living. Australian universities are known for their innovative teaching methods, research facilities, and strong international reputation, which makes them attractive to employers worldwide. Additionally, Australia is a safe and welcoming country with a diverse population that offers many opportunities for personal and professional growth.

Education system in Australia:

Australia has a world-renowned education system, with over 40 universities and a wide range of vocational education and training (VET) institutions. The Australian education system offers qualifications at all levels of study, from certificate level to doctoral degrees. The system is divided into three main sectors: primary and secondary education, vocational education and training (VET), and higher education.

Lifestyle and cost of living in Australia:

Australia is a vibrant and diverse country that offers a high standard of living. The cost of living varies depending on the location and lifestyle of the student. Major cities such as Sydney and Melbourne are more expensive than smaller cities and towns. The average cost of living in Australia is around AUD 20,000 to 25,000 per year, which includes accommodation, food, transport, and other expenses. However, international students are allowed to work part-time while studying, which can help cover some of their expenses.

Additional information:

It's important to note that international students must have a valid student visa to study in Australia. The visa application process can take several weeks or months, so it's important to plan ahead. Additionally, students must have adequate health insurance coverage while studying in Australia, which can be obtained through their institution or a private provider. Finally, it's a good idea for students to research their chosen institution and course thoroughly before applying to ensure it meets their needs and goals.

Required Documents to Apply for an Australian Visa

Usually, the following documents are required by Nepalese students to apply for a student visa for Australia. Please note that these are only for reference. As the Australian high commission keeps changing visa policies and required documents for it, we highly encourage you to visit our office for the most updated and first hand information.

  1. A valid passport.
  2. Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from an Australian institution.
  3. Evidence of financial capacity to support your stay in Australia, such as bank statements or a letter from a sponsor.
  4. English language proficiency test results (such as IELTS, PTE or TOEFL).
  5. Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the duration of your stay.
  6. Completed visa application form (online or paper)
  7. Passport-sized photographs

Steps to apply for a student visa to Australia:

Here are steps for applying an Australian student visa. Please keep in mind that these are for reference purposes only. Australia constantly updates it immigration policies. Therefore, we reccomend to do a fresh research yourself or visit our office for expert guidance.

  1. Choose your course and institution: Research the courses and institutions that suit your needs and preferences, and obtain a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from your chosen institution.
  2. Check the visa requirements: Ensure that you meet all the requirements for the student visa, including the Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement, English language proficiency, and financial capacity.
  3. Create an ImmiAccount: Create an ImmiAccount on the Australian Government's Department of Home Affairs website and fill out the student visa application form.
  4. Upload supporting documents: Upload all the required documents, including your CoE, financial documents, and English language proficiency test results.
  5. Pay the visa fee: Pay the application fee for your student visa. The current fee is AUD 620 (as of April 2023), but it's important to check the current fee on the Department of Home Affairs website.
  6. Attend a biometrics appointment: You may be required to attend a biometrics appointment to provide your fingerprints and a photograph.
  7. Wait for your visa: Once your application has been processed and approved, you will receive your student visa. The processing time may vary, but it typically takes around four to six weeks.

It's important to note that the visa application process can be complex and time-consuming, so it's a good idea to start early and seek guidance from a reputable migration agent or education consultant if needed.
